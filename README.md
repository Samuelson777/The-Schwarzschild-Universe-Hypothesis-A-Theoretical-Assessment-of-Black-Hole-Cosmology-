## Overview
This repository hosts the manuscript and supporting theoretical materials for the paper *"The Schwarzschild Universe Hypothesis: A Theoretical Assessment of Black Hole Cosmology."* The research evaluates a radical alternative to the standard ΛCDM cosmological model, synthesizing historical literature and modern theoretical physics to analyze the mathematical viability of the observable universe existing within the interior of a supermassive black hole.

## Core Theoretical Concepts
* **The Schwarzschild-Hubble Synchronicity:** The mathematical alignment where the Schwarzschild radius of the universe's mass is nearly identical to the current Hubble radius (r_s = 2GM / c²).
* **Einstein-Cartan Gravity:** The application of spacetime torsion and intrinsic fermion spin to theoretically prevent a spacetime singularity, generating a cosmological "bounce" instead of a Big Bang.
* **The Holographic Principle:** Modeling cosmic expansion as the growth of a black hole's event horizon surface area as it incorporates mass/information from a parent universe.

## Conclusion
Current observational data neither definitively prove nor disprove the Black Hole Cosmology hypothesis. The mathematical synchronization between the universe's mass, its radius, and the Schwarzschild metric presents a compelling theoretical framework. Furthermore, Einstein-Cartan gravity provides a mathematically viable mechanism for a universe-creating bounce. 

However, due to limitations in observational verification—specifically regarding the uniform temperature of the Cosmic Microwave Background (CMB) and a lack of distinct falsifiability—the standard ΛCDM model remains the consensus framework. Continued exploration of the Schwarzschild Universe is vital, as it forces theoretical physics to evolve, probing the ultimate unification of quantum mechanics and gravitational physics.
